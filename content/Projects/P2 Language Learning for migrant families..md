## Secondary research

#### Competitive analysis

###### Findings (Key Features Highlights)
- Learning Progress reports
- Use of content like movies, music, stories
- Printable worksheets (offline learning)
- Bite-sized learning & +ve reinforcement  
- Maximize sensory learning

###### Problems (Further scope)
- Including cultural context, talking about local points of interest.
- Engaging with surroundings to assist learning
- Engaging with Parents/Family






## Primary Research

#### Interviews

###### Set 1 Findings (19-23y/o persons who have migrated as children and have grown up in a new state/ have spent childhood as migrants)
- The main motivators for learning languages are Academic, Pragmatic and Social.
- Daily interaction with local language speakers is most effective.
- Practicing with Parents & Friends can encourage with motivation and build familiarity
- Languages are really necessary to build connections with friends at schools(This differs among CBSE, ICSE and state schools) In all cases they make it easier none the less.
- Schooling can help with reading and writing but without any daily speaking practice, speaking isn't tackled.
- Every-day phrases is a really good starting point for language learning.


###### Set 2 Findings (Parents and children 8-15, families moved to Bengaluru from other states)
- Parents trust teachers more than apps, although some parent see sending children to additional classes as a hassle.
- In younger children parents are worried about children not being able to make new friends, where as in slightly older children academics takes precedent.
- Most parents and children have tried kannada learning apps but never end up sticking to them.
