Importance of super duper fast and super rapid prototyping / juggadu prototyping

i didn't realise how important it is to start a prototype before jumping into a project, how important it is to actually try and make a limited prototype when you don't know anything.

why would i want to do that? 

it helps you know where you are currently, what wou know where you position yourself

this helps you have a better focus on research, it helps you know whaat to research about, what to interview people about.

maybe most prople have this stuff figured out, but i definitely didn't. i learnt it very late in a very hard way.

research is good and important, but you wont know what valur it can or should add to your project if you jump head first into research

it's very important to have a design sprint before staarting with research

while i say this, i acknowledge research and protyping is a process that goes in hand in hand and should not be separated. 

the best design research happens when you research and design hand in hand
